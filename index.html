Dance

Yes, you're correct. I completed research on exotic tribal dances for my post-doctoral work. In response to your query, the following dances are some of my favorites, with definitions:

Chicken dancing in Polynesia

  • A complete town would dance around in a circle like chickens to urge their animals or be "fruitful" in 300 BC, a little-known but incredibly influential dance.

The Brownian shuffle in Iceland

  • Before they discovered how to utilize fire to stay warm, the Icelanders practiced this dance, which required huddling closely together in a circle on the ground and moving their bodies in imperceptibly small, extremely quick movements. One of my fellow pupils once claimed that he believed this dance was the source of contemporary movements like Twerking.

Dancing arctic robot

  • An interesting example of historical misinformation is the idea that a new dance form, being practiced by people in Northern Alaska and Canada, was found by English explorers in the 1960s. This dance form was characterized by "robotic," stilted gestures. However, it was later revealed that they were simply moving in this manner due to their extreme cold.
